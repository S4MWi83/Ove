Vision & Product Concept

The investing app
Europe has been waiting for.

Most Europeans keep their savings in a bank account where inflation quietly eats away at it year after year. Ové is a simple way to put that money to work instead — no jargon, no complexity, no bank switching. Just a straightforward way to finally start.

The Problem

320 million Europeans haven't started investing. Yet.

Only 9% of Europeans own stock directly (ECB). In the US it's 21%. The gap isn't money or knowledge — it's friction. A 2026 ESMA report confirms it: multiple regulatory and non-regulatory barriers combine to stop people before they even open an app.

And when they do open an app? ETFs. CFDs. Derivatives. Margin trading. A sign-up process that feels like switching banks. They close it within 5 minutes. Every time.

Target User

The person who keeps saying someday.

They have a stable income and money sitting in a savings account they're quietly embarrassed by. They know they should be investing. They've googled it. They've maybe even downloaded an app or two and deleted them within a week.

They're not lazy. They're not broke. They're overwhelmed — by jargon, by choice, by the fear of doing something wrong with their own money.

Age

18–55 — old enough to have income, young enough that starting today still matters.

Long enough horizon for compounding to actually work in their favour.

Capacity

Maybe not thousands to begin with. €50–€200/month feels right.

€100/month at 8% over 10 years is €18,000. That's the story Ové tells them.

Awareness

Knows inflation exists. Has heard of ETFs. Stops there.

Enough to feel guilty about not investing. Not enough to know where to start.

Friction

Zero tolerance. One confusing screen and they're out.

This is the entire design constraint. Every product decision runs through it.

Geography

Low-participation markets — Italy, Spain, Eastern Europe — where ownership sits well below the European average.

The opportunity is biggest where it's been ignored longest.

The Solution

Three steps. Then you're investing.

Ové removes every unnecessary choice from the onboarding experience. Regulatory compliance — MiFID II, KYC, AML — is handled invisibly. The user never sees a form they don't understand.

1

Sign up — smooth and fast

Nothing like opening a bank account. First name, email, done. KYC handled seamlessly in the background.

2

Pick a risk level

Steady, Balanced, or Growth. Three options — not three hundred. Ové explains each in plain English.

3

You're invested

Ové handles everything else automatically. Your money is working. You're done.

User Journey

Beginner today. Hands-off investor in 6 months.

Ové is built around a clear emotional arc — not just a product, but a path.

"6 months ago you took your first step. Your money has been working ever since. Ready for the next level?"

— The Ové Adventure unlock notification

After 6 consecutive months of investing, users unlock Ové Adventure — a milestone that marks the transition from beginner to confident investor. From here, they can optionally activate Ové Advisor: a full robo-advisor for €4.99/month that automatically rebalances the portfolio, adjusts risk as markets shift, and optimises for long-term growth. Zero decisions required.

  • Ové — Start simple. Pick 1 of 3 options, invest from €10, no jargon. Month 0.
  • Ové Adventure — Unlock after 6 months. A reward for showing up consistently.
  • Ové Advisor — Optional add-on at €4.99/mo. Automated, intelligent, hands-off portfolio management.

Vision

From someday to today. From today to always.

Every European who wants to invest but hasn't started yet — that's our user. Not the trader. Not the expert. The person who keeps saying someday. We turn someday into today. And today into a habit. And a habit into a fully automated, intelligent portfolio that works while they sleep.

Ové isn't just a beginner app. It's the beginning of a lifetime of investing — with a clear, rewarding path from the first €10 to a hands-off portfolio managed by Ové Advisor.

And to be clear about what that means in practice:
