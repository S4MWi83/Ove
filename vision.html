Vision & Product Concept

The investing app
Europe has been waiting for.

Most Europeans keep their savings in a bank account where inflation quietly eats away at it year after year. Ové is a simple way to put that money to work instead — no jargon, no complexity, no bank switching. Just a straightforward way to finally start.

The Problem

320 million Europeans haven't started investing. Yet.

Only 9% of Europeans own stock directly (ECB). In the US it's 21%. The gap isn't money or knowledge — it's friction. A 2026 ESMA report confirms it: multiple regulatory and non-regulatory barriers combine to stop people before they even open an app.

And when they do open an app? ETFs. CFDs. Derivatives. Margin trading. A sign-up process that feels like switching banks. They close it within 5 minutes. Every time.

The Solution

Three steps. Then you're investing.

Ové removes every unnecessary choice from the onboarding experience. Regulatory compliance is handled invisibly so the user never has to think about it.

1

Sign up — smooth and fast

Nothing like opening a bank account. First name, email, done. KYC handled seamlessly in the background.

2

Pick a risk level

Steady, Balanced, or Growth. Three options — not three hundred. Ové explains each in plain English.

3

You're invested

Ové handles everything else automatically. Your money is working. You're done.

User Journey

Beginner today. Experienced investor in 6 months.

After 6 consecutive months of investing, users unlock Ové Experienced — optional access to individual stocks and ETFs alongside their core portfolio. It's never forced. Just available when they're ready.

Regulatory Compliance

Compliance as a feature, not a checkbox.

Regulatory complexity is one of the biggest barriers to investing in Europe (ESMA 2026). Ové absorbs this entirely on behalf of the user. MiFID II compliance, KYC, AML — all handled invisibly. The user never sees a form they don't understand.

This is a core product principle, not an afterthought. It's also a key trust signal — especially for the 91% who are wary of new financial services.
