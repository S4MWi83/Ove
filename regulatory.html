Pre-launch status: Ové is not currently authorised to provide investment services. The features described here are conceptual and will only be available once Ové partners with an authorised investment firm under MiFID II. The exact regulatory structure and partner arrangements are subject to change.

The framework

Operating under MiFID II via a licensed partner.

Ové is designed to operate through a licensed brokerage partner under MiFID II — the EU directive that governs investment services and sets the standards for investor protection. Rather than seeking a standalone licence, Ové will partner with an already-authorised firm that provides the regulatory and compliance infrastructure.

This means regulatory obligations — including KYC, AML, suitability assessments, and cost disclosures — are met at the partner level before Ové ever touches a user's money.

Design principles

Simplicity without removing safeguards.

Ové's entire UX premise is that complexity is the barrier to investing — not regulation. The goal is to simplify the experience without reducing the protections users would be entitled to through an authorised partner.

Suitability

Matched to the user

Any future investment service will include appropriateness and suitability assessments as required under MiFID II — ensuring the product matches each user's knowledge, experience, and financial situation.

Cost transparency

Fees disclosed upfront

All fees — including the 0.5% AUM fee and optional Ové Advisor subscription — will be clearly disclosed before any investment is made, in line with MiFID II cost disclosure requirements.

Risk disclosure

Honest about downside

Capital is at risk. Ové will surface risk disclosures prominently — not buried in footnotes. Users will understand that investments can go down as well as up before they commit.

No behavioural nudging

No FOMO, no urgency

Ové does not use countdown timers, scarcity messaging, or return-based gamification. Regulators are increasingly focused on behavioural design — Ové is built to pass that scrutiny.

Investor protection

What users would be entitled to.

Where applicable, users would be entitled to the following protections, expected to be provided through an authorised partner under MiFID II:

  • A suitability or appropriateness assessment before investing
  • Clear disclosure of all costs and charges
  • A Key Information Document (KID) under PRIIPs before any packaged investment is made
  • The right to complain to a national competent authority
  • Client assets would be held and safeguarded by an authorised third-party provider

Data protection

GDPR-first from day one.

Ové collects only what it needs and handles data in accordance with GDPR. Users will be provided with a full privacy notice before any data collection begins. Read our Privacy Policy for full details.

Why this experience is limited

Intentional restraint, not technical limitation.

Ové does not simulate real investment decisions, execute transactions, or provide personalised outputs. This is a deliberate choice.

Investment services in the EU require regulatory authorisation, suitability or appropriateness assessments, and investor protections that are not yet in place. Offering a more realistic simulation before these safeguards exist would risk misleading users about the nature of investing and about what Ové currently is.

This approach is designed to ensure full compliance before launch — not to minimise effort, but to protect users.
